Mahabir Pun

69 years (January 22, 1955)

Teacher and social entrepreneur

Mahabir Pun is a prominent Nepalese teacher, social entrepreneur, and activist renowned for his innovative use of wireless technology to empower remote communities. His efforts have focused on bridging the digital divide in rural Nepal, promoting education, and improving access to information. Pun founded the Himanchal Education Foundation, which has been pivotal in providing educational opportunities through technology.His work has earned him global recognition, including induction into the Internet Hall of Fame for his contributions to the advancement of internet connectivity in underserved areas. In addition to his technological innovations, Mahabir Pun has been a tireless advocate for social change in Nepal. He has worked on numerous projects to enhance local infrastructure, healthcare, and agriculture in rural areas. One of his notable initiatives is the creation of the National Innovation Center (NIC), which fosters research, development, and technological innovation in Nepal. The NIC focuses on finding practical solutions to local challenges, ranging from agriculture to health and education, while also nurturing talent and supporting entrepreneurs in the country. Pun's work is widely regarded as an inspiring example of how technology and innovation can be harnessed to drive sustainable development in underprivileged regions. His dedication to uplifting rural communities and addressing the digital divide has made him a celebrated figure both in Nepal.
